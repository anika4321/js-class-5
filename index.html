The ocean: A huge body of salt water that covers most of Earth


This National Geographic Education article explains that the ocean is a vital part of
life on Earth, influencing weather, temperature, and the carbon cycle. It also contains 97%
of the Earth's water and is home to some of the world's oldest creatures.


Here are some facts about the ocean:

.The ocean covers 71% of the Earth's surface.
.The ocean contains almost 98% of all the water on Earth.
.The ocean is made up of one global ocean, but is traditionally divided
into five main areas: the Pacific, Atlantic, Indian, Arctic, and Southern oceans.
.The ocean floor is moving.
.The ocean is home to some of the world's oldest creatures, including jellyfish and
horseshoe crabs.
.The ocean is threatened by human activities, including plastic pollution,
lost fishing nets, and ships spilling oil and garbage.
